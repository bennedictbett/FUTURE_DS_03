# Funnel Analysis Report
## Olist Marketing Funnel — Jun 2017 to May 2018

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This report analyzes the Olist marketing funnel covering 7,999 leads
generated between June 2017 and May 2018. The overall conversion rate
was 10.51%, with 841 leads closing as won deals. The analysis reveals
significant growth in 2018, critical channel performance gaps, and
clear opportunities to improve conversion rates across the funnel.

## 3. Funnel Analysis

### 3.1 Overall Funnel
The funnel shows a steep drop from leads to closed won:
```
Leads        7,999   100.00%
Closed Won     841    10.51%
Drop Off     7,158    89.49%
```

89.49% of leads never converted. This is the single biggest
opportunity in the entire funnel.

### 3.2 Year Over Year
The business matured significantly between 2017 and 2018:

| Year | Leads | Won | CVR    |
|------|-------|-----|--------|
| 2017 | 2,002 | 61  | 3.05%  |
| 2018 | 5,997 | 780 | 13.01% |

CVR improved by 326% year over year. The Jan 2018 inflection point
— where CVR jumped from 5.50% to 13.32% in a single month — suggests
a significant operational or process improvement occurred at that time.

  conversion. Traffic quality from social is low.

## 6. Key Findings

1. **89.49% of leads never convert** — the funnel has a massive
   drop-off problem that needs addressing at the lead nurturing stage.

2. **UTM tracking is broken** — 1,159 leads (14.5%) have unknown
   origin. This is the highest converting segment at 16.65%, meaning
   the best channel is currently invisible.

3. **Jan 2018 inflection** — CVR jumped 7.82 percentage points in
   one month. Understanding what changed in January 2018 could unlock
   repeatable growth.

4. **Email is broken** — 493 leads, 3.04% CVR, 21 day median cycle.
   High investment, very low return.

5. **Social needs optimization** — high volume but low quality.
   Either improve targeting or reduce spend.

6. **May 2018 CVR dropped** to 9.98% from 13.54% — a 3.56pp decline
   that needs investigation before it becomes a trend.

## 7. Recommendations

### Priority 1 — Fix UTM Tracking (High Impact, Low Effort)
- Implement proper UTM parameters on all campaigns immediately
- The unknown channel converts at 16.65% — highest of all channels
- Identifying this source could significantly increase ROI
- Expected impact: full visibility into best performing channel

### Priority 2 — Fix Email Strategy (High Impact, Medium Effort)
- Current CVR of 3.04% is unacceptable for a direct channel
- Audit email sequences, subject lines, and targeting
- Segment email list by lead score before sending
- Expected impact: potential 2-3x improvement in email CVR

### Priority 3 — Scale Organic and Paid Search (High Impact)
- Both channels have strong CVR (11.76% and 12.30%)
- Increase content marketing and SEO investment for organic
- Increase paid search budget with tighter keyword targeting
- Expected impact: more high quality leads at proven CVR

### Priority 4 — Optimize Social Strategy (Medium Impact)
- Social brings 1,350 leads but only 5.56% convert
- Either improve audience targeting to attract higher intent users
- Or reduce social spend and reallocate to organic/paid search
- Expected impact: improve social CVR to 8%+ or reduce wasted spend

### Priority 5 — Investigate Jan 2018 Inflection (Strategic)
- CVR jumped from 5.50% to 13.32% in one month
- Identify what process, team, or campaign change caused this
- Document and replicate it systematically
- Expected impact: unlock repeatable growth playbook

### Priority 6 — Investigate May 2018 Drop (Urgent)
- CVR fell 3.56pp in May 2018
- Check if this is seasonal, a campaign issue, or a process failure
- Monitor June 2018 closely for continuation of the trend
- Expected impact: prevent further CVR decline
